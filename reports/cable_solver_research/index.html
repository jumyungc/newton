Round 7: ETC-VBD — the experiment-derived correction framework

Four instability experiments mapped the failure space of sparse multilevel corrections and converged on the stable design: closed-loop corrections (fresh gradient every correction; ~3 ms gradient-only assembly) against a frozen Galerkin operator (reused for 30 substeps), with merit + discrete-energy certificates. Measured knowledge: an energy-neutrality filter alone does NOT stabilize sparse cadence (blow-ups reproduce bit-for-bit); position-only corrections still diverge at 1e7; distributing stale corrections over the period is the WORST variant (open-loop forcing) — corrections must be recomputed against the current state. The energy certificate binds only at the stiffest regime (~1–5% of corrections rejected at 1e7, improving stretch 37%).

Round 8: spectral trigger — pay for global help only when the error is coarse

The trigger asks "is the violation coarse?" (span-mean violation coherence, ~1.5 ms), not "is it big?", plus a futility cooldown when corrections stop paying. Measured: on the soft 1e4 scene it fires only 207/600 substeps and the solver runs at 0.69 ms/substep — VBD2 cost — while still improving stretch (6.8 vs 10.3 mm); on whips it correctly stays always-on with full ETC accuracy (0.134 mm at 1e6). Escalation (64 coarse nodes, two sweeps) improves the extreme rung to 0.284 mm at 1e7 (1 s) at unchanged ~27 ms, and 7.09 mm over the full 5 s whip (vs 8.71) — the extreme-stiffness long horizon remains the full global solve's crown (0.099 mm), now an explicit routing rule rather than an open question. Verdict of the adaptive portfolio: VBD2 always → spectral trigger → certified closed-loop coarse correction → escalate coarse DOFs/sweeps → full global solve for extreme-stiffness long horizons, all certificate-gated. Manifests: spectral/escalation 5-s horizon.

Round 9: full-registry certification — both-channel trigger + passivity fuse

ETC-spectral applied to every chain-topology benchmark scene (301 frames, initial states hash-verified identical to the round-4 VBD2/VBD80/GLOBAL baselines); branched/loop scenes are routed to the full GLOBAL solve per the portfolio rule. Two mechanisms are new and both came out of negative results: (1) the coherence trigger now watches both violation channels — the stretch-only trigger was blind to bend-driven long-range error and under-fired on soft-stretch/stiff-bend whips; (2) a trajectory-level passivity fuse — a control experiment showed the coarse correction itself pumps energy on extreme bend-anisotropy whips even when fired every substep (647 mm) and even with a fresh coarse operator every fire (128 mm; operator staleness exonerated), while every per-substep certificate passes. Since these scenes are passive, a windowed total-energy rise above the initial level while corrections run is proof of corrector-driven pumping: the fuse then disables corrections for the rest of the episode. It converts 308–1706 mm trajectory corruption into bounded 100–178 mm, automatically, with zero scene-specific tuning.
