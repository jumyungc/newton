Verification Target

This test isolates the bend/twist split in the simplest setting with exact answers. Root and tip bodies prescribe either a constant-curvature bend arc or a pure tangent-axis twist; VBD solves the interior bodies and must put the deformation into the matching subspace only.

This is the baseline accuracy gate for the split. Later reports add loads, history, routed geometry, and ring instabilities.

Video Checks

The videos are visual checks for the two exact boundary-value solutions. Bend focus compares the VBD centerline to the exact discrete arc. Twist inspection compares simulated material-frame phase to exact linear phase in a deliberately high 150-degree pure-twist case.

Acceptance Criteria

Passes When

  • Blue/green bend overlays coincide for the 30-, 60-, and 90-degree rows
  • Orange/cyan twist ticks stay aligned in the 150-degree visual stress case
  • The twist centerline remains straight while the material frame rotates

Watch For

  • Visible gaps open between the blue and green bend overlays
  • Orange twist ticks roll ahead of or behind the cyan reference
  • The twist row bows, shifts sideways, or loses straightness

Validated Behavior

  • Constant-curvature bend reaches the exact discrete arc
  • Pure twist stays straight and follows the exact linear twist profile
  • Bend and twist stay isolated in full SolverVBD stepping
